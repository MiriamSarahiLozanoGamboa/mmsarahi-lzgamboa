About

Welcome to my personal website! I am a dedicated and passionate researcher with a strong background in biotechnology and molecular biology. My journey began with a Bachelor’s degree in Biotechnology from the Autonomous University of Aguascalientes. Building on this, I pursued a Master’s degree in Molecular Biology at the Potosino Institute of Scientific and Technological Research (IPICyT), focusing on the fascinating field of cancer research and multidrug resistance.

My master’s thesis explored the role of diterpenes derived from Jatropha dioica in modulating multidrug resistance in human breast carcinoma cell lines. This research not only deepened my expertise in experimental techniques but also ignited my passion for understanding complex biological systems and their interactions with therapeutic agents.

Experience

Currently, I am working at the Laboratorio Estatal de Salud Pública (ISSEA) in molecular epidemiology, focusing on projects related to antibiotic-resistant bacteria and their genomic profiles. My work involves utilizing advanced sequencing technologies to analyze the resistomes of various bacterial strains, which is crucial for understanding the spread and evolution of multidrug-resistant pathogens.

Sequencing plays a vital role in epidemiology by providing detailed insights into the genetic makeup of resistant bacteria. This enables us to track resistance mechanisms, identify emerging threats, and implement targeted interventions. Our research is further enhanced through collaboration with the National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM) and Dr. Corina Ceapa, whose expertise in bioinformatics and genomics significantly contributes to our understanding of the complex interactions between bacterial resistance and host factors. Dr. Ceapa's work, detailed on her ResearchGate profile, provides valuable insights into the molecular mechanisms underpinning resistance, helping to inform public health strategies and improve treatment outcomes.
