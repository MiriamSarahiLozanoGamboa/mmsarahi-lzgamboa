Chemotherapy Resistance

This master's thesis project was supervised by Dr. Francisco E. Bautista Redonda and Dr. Luis A. Salazar Olivo at IPICyT. The objective was to verify if the terpenes derived from the Jatropha dioica plant had the capacity to modulate resistance to chemotherapy in resistant breast cells.

The project included a research stay at UNAM in Mexico City, supervised by Dr. Rogelio Pereda Miranda. During the stay, I cultivated cell lines of different cancers, including colon, cervix, prostate, and mouse leukemia. The focus was on vinblastine-resistant breast cancer cells (MCF-7), developed by our team, and exploring the potential of plants used in traditional Mexican medicine to discover new therapeutic targets.

COVID-19 Mutations

During the sequencing of COVID-19 strains from deceased patients, we found that the Delta variant was the most prevalent lineage associated with deaths in 2021-2022. The B.1.1.529 lineages emerged in mid-2022, and currently, the XBB lineage is the most commonly identified in Mexico.

New mutations L95M and L46M in ORF 8 and ORF 9 were discovered in 30% and 20% of the sequences, respectively. These mutations are positively associated with patient death and provide crucial insights into the evolution of SARS-CoV-2 in the region.
